How I Secured Employement During Covid

Sameen Salam 26 December, 2020

What a wild year it's been. The illustrious year of 2020 threw everything but the kitchen sink at us. Almost everyone has dealt with the large-scale effects of this pandemic, from a destroyed economy to a prolonged period of social isolation. Additionally, we've all dealt with our own pandemic-induced personal problems, ranging from the premature passing of loved ones, to cancelled events and milestones, to serious professional setbacks. As this year draws to a close, I want to reflect on my experiences and share my tips in finding employment during the worst pandemic that my generation will (hopefully) ever see.


First, a little background around my situation circa COVID. Back in March, I was still taking classes and wrapping up my M.S. in Analytics at NC State. Luckily for me, the curriculum was structured in a way that classes ended right around when the statewide lockdowns began. I only had to contend with two days worth of Zoom University. However, most of the employers that come to our program during the March-May timeline to network/interview students as part of our internal process cancelled all plans indefintely, which was a blow to people like me who hadn't yet secured any employment. As much as it sucked in the moment, I honestly believed that this whole thing would be over and done with in 2-3 weeks. If only.
